Syntropic Adaptation Cycle

Perception (Inference)

In the Perception stage, we collaborate with your organization to understand your current beliefs and predictions about your business environment, creating a “generative model” of how your business functions. Together, we interpret new data to update your understanding using advanced analytics and Bayesian inference techniques. This iterative process refines your knowledge of the hidden drivers in your business landscape, enabling more informed predictions and adaptive strategies.

Action (Policy Selection)

In the Action stage, we help you formulate and select strategic actions that minimize the gap between your predictions and actual outcomes, while maximizing the achievement of your business objectives. We guide you in choosing actions that test your assumptions, reduce uncertainty, and maintain a coherent understanding of your business environment.

Learning (Model Updating)

As your organization implements strategic actions and observes their impacts, we help you continuously refine your generative model to better reflect the structure and dynamics of your business environment. Through Bayesian model updating and other advanced techniques, we assist you in learning from prediction errors and adjusting your model parameters. Over time, your organization's understanding becomes more accurate and sophisticated, enabling better predictions and adaptation to changing circumstances.

The Syntropic Adaptation Cycle operates continuously, with each component informing and influencing the others in a closed-loop approach. This ongoing cycle gives organizations the capacity to actively explore and exploit its environment, to test and refine its hypotheses, and to develop increasingly accurate and adaptive models of the world.

The Syntropic Process

  • Belief Mapping: A process of explicitly identifying and mapping current beliefs, assumptions, and mental models about a given situation or problem.
  • Uncertainty Calibration: Techniques for assessing and quantifying the level of uncertainty associated with different beliefs and outcomes, to prioritize areas for exploration and learning.
  • Proactive Exploration: Strategies for actively seeking out new information and experiences that can help refine beliefs and reduce uncertainty, such as experiments, simulations, or targeted data collection.
  • Bayesian Belief Updating: A structured approach to updating beliefs based on new evidence, using Bayesian inference to integrate prior knowledge with observed data.
  • Expectation-Driven Action: Guidelines for translating updated beliefs into goal-directed actions, by generating and testing predictions about the likely outcomes of different choices.
  • Iterative Refinement: An emphasis on continuously iterating between belief updating and action, to adapt to changing circumstances and improve performance over time.
  • Metacognitive Reflection: Practices for reflecting on one's own thought processes and learning experiences, to identify biases, blind spots, and areas for improvement.
